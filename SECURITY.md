# Security Policy

We take the security of dev953 seriously, and we're grateful to anyone who takes
the time to report a problem responsibly.

## Reporting a vulnerability

Please do not open a public issue for security problems.

Report privately, in either of these ways:

- Email **aninda.sh.15@gmail.com** with the details, or
- Open a **GitHub private security advisory** on the repository
  (Security tab -> Report a vulnerability).

A useful report includes what you found, where, the impact, and the steps to
reproduce it. A small proof of concept helps a lot.

## What to expect

- **Acknowledgement within 72 hours.** We will confirm we received your report
  and let you know we're looking into it.
- **A coordinated-disclosure window of up to 90 days.** We'll work with you on a
  timeline to understand, fix, and release.
- **Fixes ship before public disclosure.** We release the patched version first,
  then publish the advisory.
- **Reporters get credit.** Advisories credit the people who reported the issue,
  unless you'd prefer to stay anonymous. Just let us know.

## Safe harbour

We consider security research carried out in good faith under this policy to be
authorized. If you make a genuine effort to follow it -- report privately, avoid
privacy violations and data destruction, don't degrade the service for others,
and give us reasonable time to respond -- we will not pursue or support legal
action against you for that research. If you're unsure whether something is in
scope, ask first at aninda.sh.15@gmail.com and we'll be glad to help.

Thank you for helping keep dev953 and its users safe.
